In a four-player squad, avoid having everyone run pure damage builds. Assign one player to crowd control using freeze or slow effects, another to high single-target boss damage, and the remaining players to area-of-effect clear and utility support skills like turrets or healing.

<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide: Surviving the Deep-Sea Run">

Entering the sunken ruins for the first time can be overwhelming. The procedural sequencing of handcrafted rooms means that while the layout changes with every run, the core combat rules remain consistent. Success in Abyssus requires mastering movement, understanding upgrade priorities, and learning boss attack patterns.

Follow this step-by-step progression path to establish a solid foundation for your early expeditions:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Loadout Purpose">
    Before starting a run, decide if you are focusing on solo survival or team synergy. Choose a starting weapon and active skill that complement each other. If playing co-op, make sure your loadout does not duplicate your teammates' roles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master Constant Movement">
    Standing still is the quickest way to end a run. Use your dash, double-jump, and arena verticality to stay mobile. Keep your distance from exploding enemies and save your movement tools to dodge large area-of-effect attacks from bosses.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Prioritize Key Upgrades">
    As you clear rooms, you will encounter upgrade stations, altars, and chests. Do not chase raw damage exclusively. Early in a run, prioritize survivability upgrades, health restoration, and ability cooldown reductions to ensure you reach the deeper sectors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Learn Boss Attack Cycles">
    Bosses like Golemancer, Kri'Su, and Un'glu use highly structured attack patterns, including invulnerability phases and hazard rings. Focus on dodging their attacks first, and only commit to heavy damage during their recovery windows.
  </Step>
</Steps>
