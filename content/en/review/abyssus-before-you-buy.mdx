<Section icon={Target} title="The Core Loop: Mastering the Brinepunk Roguelite">

Understanding the gameplay loop of **Abyssus before you buy** is crucial. Unlike traditional linear shooters, Abyssus utilizes a roguelite structure where death returns you to the hub, but your meta-progression remains intact. Each expedition takes you through a series of handcrafted rooms that are procedurally sequenced, meaning the layout of individual combat arenas is intentional, but the order in which you encounter them changes every run.

The combat is defined by high mobility. Players have access to dashes, double-jumps, and vertical arena spacing. Standing still is a death sentence, especially when dealing with the 36 different enemy types that utilize everything from melee rushes to complex projectile patterns.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Loadout">
    Choose your primary weapon and initial skill at the hub. In co-op, coordinate with your team to ensure you have a mix of area clear and single-target damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Navigate the Abyss">
    Fight through combat arenas and complete objectives. Look for upgrade stations and altars to gain temporary power-ups for the current run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stack Blessings & Charms">
    Collect elemental Blessings (Fire, Ice, Lightning) to modify your fire modes. Combine these with Charms that offer passive stat boosts or mechanical changes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defeat the Boss">
    Face one of the 12 massive bosses. These fights require pattern recognition and precise movement to survive their "Surge" phases.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Meta-Progression">
    Use collected resources to unlock new weapons, permanent skill tree nodes, and Ancient Forge mods for future expeditions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Movement is Survival">
Treat your dash and double-jump as your primary defensive tools. Most boss attacks in Abyssus are designed to be dodged through verticality or timed dashes rather than simply moving out of the way.
</Callout>

</Section>
