<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding the Deep Water 30 Challenge">

Reaching **abyssus deep water 30** is a significant milestone that separates casual players from endgame veterans. At this level of corruption, enemies possess massive health pools and can easily overwhelm you if your build lacks synergy. Success is not just about raw damage; it is about managing resources, choosing the right forge upgrades, and knowing which corruptions to disable to keep the run manageable.

<Callout type="warning" title="Corruption Management">
Since 30 is four levels below the maximum corruption, you have the luxury of removing four specific negative modifiers. We highly recommend removing **Royal Heralds**, as they act as massive resource drains that can ruin a run right before the final boss.
</Callout>

When planning your run, you must decide on a primary damage engine. Whether you prefer the precision of a bow or the chaotic room-clearing potential of the Brine Field, your choice of weapon and forge mods will dictate your entire strategy.

| Removal Priority | Corruption Name | Reason for Removal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High** | Royal Heralds | Prevents massive HP/resource loss before bosses |
| **High** | Melee Execute | Useless for ranged builds; high risk for low reward |
| **Medium** | Healing Shrine Syringes | Limits sustain, but manageable with high Barrier |
| **Medium** | Post-Boss Healing | Forces perfect play; remove if your defense is low |

<Callout type="tip" title="Sustainability Tip">
If you choose to keep the "No Healing after Boss" corruption, you must prioritize the **Barrier** blessing. It acts as a regenerating shield that prevents permanent HP loss from chip damage.
</Callout>

### 1. The Combat Bow (Infinite Scaling)
The Bow is arguably the strongest weapon for high-level corruption due to the **Stable String** forge upgrade. This upgrade allows each consecutive weakspot hit to increase damage by 10%, stacking up to 25 times. When paired with **True Form**, which makes sweetspots always count as weakspots, your damage can reach into the millions within seconds.

### 2. The Shotgun (Slug Specialist)
The Shotgun build relies on transforming the weapon from a spread-based tool into a high-precision cannon using **Super Heavy Slugs**. Because slugs remove the spread mechanic, you can take upgrades like **Sawed-Off Barrel** (+50% damage, +100% spread) without any actual accuracy penalty.

| Weapon Component | Recommended Mod | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bow Primary** | Precision Shot | Increases base weakspot multiplier |
| **Bow Forge 1** | Stable String | 10% dmg per hit, stacks to 25x |
| **Bow Forge 2** | True Form | Sweetspots = Weakspot hits |
| **Shotgun Primary**| Super Heavy Slug | High damage single projectile |
| **Shotgun Forge 1**| Light Frame | +40% Fire Rate, +80% Move Speed |

<Callout type="success" title="Build Synergy">
For the Shotgun build, focus all your leveling efforts on primary fire. While turrets are useful, at Deep Water 30, your primary slug damage will far outpace any secondary ability damage.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Essential Blessings and Amulet Synergies">

Your choice of blessings can make or break an **abyssus deep water 30** run. While damage is important, survivability and utility often provide more consistency in the "Deep Water" layers.

### Blessing Priorities
- **Barrier (Ancient Cube)**: This is the gold standard for survival. It provides a buffer that absorbs damage, allowing you to make mistakes without losing permanent health.
- **Frost (Secondary)**: Freezing enemies is the best form of crowd control. It stops mobs in their tracks and gives you time to align weakspot shots.
- **Shadow (Primary)**: Applies a debuff that increases the damage enemies take from all sources. This is vital for taking down high-HP elite units.

<Callout type="info" title="Brine Field Strategy">
If you prefer a "lazy" build, go all-in on **Brine Field** with the **Freebie** and **Quality over Quantity** forge mods. This creates a massive field that clears entire rooms automatically while you focus on dodging.
</Callout>

### Amulet Tier List
Amulets provide passive buffs that scale your power exponentially as the run progresses.

| Amulet Name | Tier | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thief's Eye** | S | Double loot from chests; speeds up power spikes |
| **Blessed** | S | +10% damage for each Blessing you own |
| **Slow Power** | A | +1% damage every 2 seconds, up to +50% |
| **Marksman** | A | +1% damage per hit, resets on miss |

<Callout type="tip" title="Economy Tip">
Always pick **Thief's Eye** if it appears in the first two zones. The extra blessings and forge materials you get from doubled chests will make the final 10 corruption levels significantly easier.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Run Progression">

To successfully clear **abyssus deep water 30**, you need a disciplined approach to leveling and resource management. Follow these steps to ensure your build is ready for the final boss.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Early Game Setup">
    Focus on obtaining your core weapon and primary blessing. If you are using the Bow, look for **Precision Shot**. If using the Shotgun, secure **Super Heavy Slugs**. Don't worry about defense yet; focus on clearing rooms fast to gather materials.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="First Forge Upgrade">
    Prioritize your first Forge upgrade. For the Bow, this **must** be **Stable String**. For the Shotgun, look for **Light Frame** to increase your movement speed. Speed is your primary defense in the middle zones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Mid-Game Defense">
    By the second zone, you should have **Barrier** at level 3 or higher. This ensures that you can survive accidental hits from projectiles. Start looking for the **Frost** blessing on your secondary ability to slow down aggressive mobs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Corruption Removal">
    As you reach the threshold of level 30, ensure you have removed the **Royal Heralds**. This prevents the "resource drain" phase that often kills runs before the final encounter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Final Scaling">
    In the final zones, dump all resources into your primary damage scaling. If you have the **Blessed** amulet, try to pick up as many cheap blessings as possible to maximize the percentage damage bonus.
  </Step>
</Steps>
