The core mystery of the game lies in its endings and what they reveal about the expedition's true purpose. In one ending, the explorer defeats the underwater god only to ascend and take its place on the throne [3]. This seemingly heroic victory is a trap. Once you claim the throne, you become the new deity of the deep, waiting for the next expedition team to descend and defeat you [3]. This creates a perfect narrative justification for the roguelike loop, where the player's victory is actually their condemnation to become the next obstacle [3].

In the second ending, the explorer defeats the god and a diving bell appears, allowing them to "complete the expedition" [3]. However, this completion does not grant true freedom. The narrative implies that the call of the deep is hypnotic, and survivors find themselves drawn back to the diving bell, unable to escape the psychological pull of the abyss [3].

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Weapons and Blessings: Lore-Infused Mechanics">

The weapons and upgrade systems in the game are not just mechanical tools; they are deeply tied to the narrative. The technology used by the expedition is a mix of high-voltage brinepunk engineering and salvaged relics. The Tesla gun, for example, represents the pinnacle of the crew's technological adaptation, utilizing the conductivity of the deep sea to chain high-damage electrical currents. The revolver, a favorite for its precision, can be modified with various charms and blessings to channel supernatural forces.

Blessings represent the influence of the ancient deities ruling the depths. These blessings are categorized into elemental forces such as Ocean, Spirit, Barrier, Lightning, and Wind. Each blessing provides a mechanical upgrade while hinting at the nature of the gods that control the environment.
