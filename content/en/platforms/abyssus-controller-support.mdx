<Section icon={Settings} title="PC Setup: Enabling Steam Input">

For PC players, ensuring your **abyssus controller support** works correctly often requires a quick trip into the Steam settings. While the game has native drivers for many devices, Steam Input acts as a universal translator for older or niche gamepads.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access Controller Settings">
    Right-click **Abyssus** in your Steam Library, select **Properties**, and navigate to the **Controller** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Override for Abyssus">
    Set the "Override for Abyssus" dropdown to **Enable Steam Input**. This ensures Steam handles the API translation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="In-Game Calibration">
    Launch the game and go to **Settings > Input**. Adjust your look sensitivity and deadzones to match your stick tension.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Layout Verification">
    Check that the on-screen prompts match your controller (e.g., ABXY for Xbox or Shapes for PlayStation).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Input Lag Tip">
If you experience "floaty" aiming on PC, disable V-Sync in the video settings. This often reduces the perceived input latency when using a controller.
</Callout>

</Section>
