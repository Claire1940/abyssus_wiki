The **abyssus disc thrower** stands out as one of the most unique and challenging weapons in the Brinehunter arsenal. Unlike the straightforward fire rate of the Engine Rifle or the raw explosive power of the Plasma Launcher, the Disc Thrower relies on spatial awareness and projectile management. In the 1.3 update, the Disc Thrower received significant tuning to its base projectile speed and return arc. Mastering this weapon requires understanding that the initial shot is only half the battle; the return trip of the disc often deals more damage if positioned correctly.

The 1.3 update introduced several new mods specifically designed to synergize with the expanded skill tree. These mods allow the Disc Thrower to act more like a control tool than a pure damage dealer, which is vital when facing the 36 different enemy types found in the deeper expeditions.

<Section icon={Map} title="Combat Tactics: Step-by-Step Mastery">

Using the **abyssus disc thrower** effectively requires more than just pulling the trigger. You must treat every room like a puzzle. The following steps outline the optimal combat flow for a standard expedition, whether you are playing solo or in a 4-player co-op squad.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Survey the Geometry">
    Before engaging, identify the walls and obstacles. Look for "choke points" where a single disc can bounce multiple times between two surfaces.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Initiate with Control">
    Use your secondary fire or a control-based ability (like the Smiting Spear) to group enemies together. This ensures your first throw impacts the maximum number of targets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Position for the Return">
    Immediately after throwing, move to a position where the returning disc will pass through the most enemies. Do not stand still; use dash and double-jump to adjust the return angle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Heat and Reloads">
    Keep an eye on your weapon's heat gauge. Overheating at the wrong time leaves you defenseless. Use the "Magnetic Catch" to reset your rhythm and keep the pressure on.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Capitalize on Boss Windows">
    During boss fights like Kri'Su or Un'glu, wait for their invulnerability phases to end. Use the disc's ricochet to hit bosses from behind their shields or armor plating.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Hazard Awareness">
Be careful in rooms with explosive hazards. A stray ricochet from your Disc Thrower can trigger barrels or brine-leaks prematurely, potentially damaging you or your teammates. Always aim away from volatiles unless you intend to detonate them.
</Callout>

In co-op play, your role is often "Area Denial." By keeping multiple discs in the air, you create a zone of danger that prevents smaller enemies from swarming your teammates. Coordinate with a player using the Tesla Gun to stun enemies, making them sitting ducks for your serrated projectiles.

</Section>
